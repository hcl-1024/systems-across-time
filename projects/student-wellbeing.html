Student Wellbeing and Support Systems

A practice-based case study of how peer participation and institutional support interact.

Research context

Community wellbeing depends on relationships, shared norms, and reliable pathways to help. Support is most effective when students can recognize and access it.

Central question

How can peer initiatives complement professional and institutional support without confusing their distinct responsibilities?

Approach and insights

The Student Wellbeing Ambassador role developed listening, discretion, and responsible communication. It showed how visible peer participation can strengthen—but should never replace—formal support structures.

Type of work

Practice-based case study

Grounded in service as a Student Wellbeing Ambassador.
