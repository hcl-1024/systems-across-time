Classical Languages and Reception

Connecting Latin language study with classical texts and the changing interpretation of antiquity.

Research context

Language study provides access to the words, institutions, and assumptions of ancient texts. Reception studies asks how later communities select, reinterpret, and use that classical past.

Central question

How does collaborative language study change the way ancient texts and their modern afterlives are understood?

Approach and insights

Translation, textual discussion, and historical context formed a shared practice through Latin Club. The setting demonstrates how community can sustain difficult learning while encouraging critical interpretation.

Type of work

Language-based inquiry

Developed through collaborative work in Latin Club.
