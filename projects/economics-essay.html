Independent Research in Political Economy

Building an economic argument from evidence, explicit assumptions, and competing explanations.

Research context

Political economy examines how economic choices interact with institutions, incentives, and distributions of power. Strong arguments make their assumptions and limits visible.

Central question

How can an open economic problem be translated into a defensible argument without concealing uncertainty or counterevidence?

Approach and insights

An independent essay developed for the John Locke Institute Essay Competition used source evaluation, structured reasoning, and counterargument. The process distinguished a persuasive claim from one that is merely attractive.

Type of work

Independent analytical research

Developed for the John Locke Institute Essay Competition.
