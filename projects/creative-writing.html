Narrative Inquiry and Creative Practice

Using narrative form to examine experience, perspective, and the structures beneath everyday life.

Research context

Narrative complements analytical research by making room for voice, ambiguity, and lived experience. Stories can show how large structures are felt within individual lives.

Central question

How can creative form reveal relationships and experiences that conventional analysis may leave implicit?

Approach and insights

Drafting and revision develop narrative structure, precision of language, and perspective-taking. Recognition by the Scholastic Art & Writing Awards marks one stage in this continuing creative practice.

Type of work

Creative inquiry

Practice-based work recognized through the Scholastic Art & Writing Awards.
