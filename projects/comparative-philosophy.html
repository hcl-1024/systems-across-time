Comparative Philosophy

Reading traditions alongside one another to reveal both shared questions and genuine differences.

Project focus

This research examines how philosophical traditions construct ideas about knowledge, ethics, identity, and human flourishing. Comparison is treated as a method of careful listening—not as a search for easy equivalence.

What the work develops

Close reading, disciplined comparison, source evaluation, and the ability to explain why historical and cultural context matters to an argument.

Guiding question

What becomes visible when systems of thought meet?

The project emphasizes interpretation across difference.
