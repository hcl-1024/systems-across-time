Urban Systems and Emerging Technologies

Considering how data, infrastructure, and engineering choices shape everyday urban life.

Research context

Urban technologies operate within existing social and physical systems. Their value depends not only on technical performance but also on access, governance, and public consequences.

Central question

How can emerging technologies respond to real urban needs without treating a city as a purely technical problem?

Approach and insights

Research and presentation at an IEEE Young Engineers Conference combined systems thinking with public communication. The work reinforced that engineering decisions distribute attention, access, and opportunity.

Type of work

Technical inquiry

Presented through an IEEE Young Engineers Conference.
