About

A portfolio built around connections

Systems Across Time brings together research, service, leadership, and creative work without flattening their differences.

Why this collection exists

The projects on this site range from Roman economic history to student wellbeing. What connects them is an interest in the systems people inherit, maintain, question, and rebuild.

The site separates short project narratives from the full research record. Visitors can browse the story here, while researchers and contributors can inspect data, definitions, and reproducible calculations in the dedicated GitHub repository.

How to use the site

Begin with the project directory for a broad view. The research section gives a guided introduction to the Cicero credit-networks study, defines O1–O6, and points to the public repository. A manuscript link will be added only after the paper is ready for public release.
