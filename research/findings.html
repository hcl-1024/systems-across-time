Interpretation

What the study makes visible

The findings distinguish direct evidence from modeled inference and preserve uncertainty as part of the result.

Networks are relational evidence

O1–O4 use graph variants to test how actors, roles, intermediaries, and mechanisms appear under different defensible representations. The results are best read as structured views of surviving evidence, not a complete map of Roman credit.

Archive dependence is a finding

Because Cicero's correspondence determines much of what survives, O2 treats that archival concentration as something to measure rather than ignore. This helps separate observed centrality from historical importance beyond the archive.

O5 and O6 estimate scale

O5 models active private-credit stock within Cicero's community. O6 extends the comparison to a wider Roman elite environment. Their distribution values are generated through a separate borrower-side workflow and should be read with their assumptions and uncertainty intervals.

Use the live research record

Numbers may change when evidence is corrected or added. The repository is the canonical location for exact outputs, release notes, and rerunnable calculations; this page provides the interpretive map.

Reading rule

Model output is not a newly discovered ancient total

Each estimate depends on stated assumptions. Reproducibility makes those assumptions inspectable.
