Research design

Six objectives, named in plain language

The labels O1–O6 are retained so the website, paper, data, and calculations use the same vocabulary. Each label is paired with a public-facing description.

O1

Actor centrality by social role

Who occupies structurally important positions, and how does that vary by role?

O2

Dependence on Cicero's archive

How much of the observed network is shaped by the surviving Ciceronian evidence?

O3

Intermediaries and third parties

How do brokers, guarantors, agents, and other third parties connect transactions?

O4

Credit mechanisms and financial pressure

Which mechanisms appear in the records, and what do they show about constraint or pressure?

O5

Active private-credit stock in Cicero's community

What scale of active private credit is supported or implied by the Ciceronian evidence?

O6

Wider Roman-elite comparison

How might the Ciceronian network compare with a broader Roman elite credit environment?
